Career Paths

Executives’ career paths are often as clear and straightforward as they are themselves. Though there are many directions they can choose to go, people with the Executive personality type almost always end up in situations where they have the opportunity to exercise their affinity for organization, structure, and follow-through. Providing further focus, Executives share a profound respect for tradition, stability and security, qualities which lend themselves well to progressing along clear paths to increased responsibility and dependability.

Executives’ sense of loyalty results in them staying with a single employer as long as possible, and their reverence for established institutions often leads to work with well-respected organizations. Law enforcement, military service, hospitals and prominent legal firms are all able to recognize Executives’ long years of service.

Executives are the image of the model citizen, and they strive to maintain this ideal throughout their careers. This helps them move forward into management as well – people with this personality type have many traits inherent to leadership, something that is often recognized regardless of their chosen industry. From the genuine enjoyment Executives feel in organizing other people to their knack for clearly expressing their principles, values and expectations, Executives are extremely effective managers.

Also fearsome leaders, Executives loathe disorganization, ineptitude, laziness and especially dishonesty, and when these values are crossed, Executive personalities do not hesitate to let their disapproval show. So long as everyone plays by the rules, Executives are incredibly efficient, their love of structure and thoroughness leaving a record of accurate, complete work that is on time and on budget. Executives are natural auditors, financial officers and business administrators, and any such and similar roles are excellent choices for them.


To the Victors Belong the Spoils

Hard work and self-motivation are also strong Executive principles, which makes them excellent sales representatives, whether in basic retail positions, part of office teams, or as independent agents. People with the Executive personality type stick to projects until they’re finished, big or small, and are organized enough to make any necessary paperwork a mere minor inconvenience to their clients, rather than some baffling ordeal. These qualities combine to make the clear steps in advancement that Executives require in order to maintain a sense of accomplishment not just viable, but almost inevitable.

Business Manager

For an in-charge ESTJ, combining a love of business with the discipline of management is a no-brainer. The business manager is in charge of the organization’s day-to-day operations. In this role, an ESTJ will manage employees, supervise work, and ensure all company policies are met, all work an ESTJ will find rewarding.

Telemarketer

Persistent, extroverted, and ready to sway someone to their side, ESTJs make successful telemarketers. Often making cold calls, telemarketers speak with potential customers, sharing information about the company or organization to sell their product. They must be able to persuade customers, close sales, and quickly move on.

Chief Information Officer

The leader and decision-maker of the company IT department, the chief information officer is a perfect role for the executive ESTJ. As CIO, the ESTJ will implement strategies and select technologies to improve their company. They are responsible for maintaining technology, IT staff, and infrastructure. They are also the go-to person to offer advice on new purchases and tech and provide guidance within the organization.

Senior Accountant

Senior accountants are principled leaders at their firms and tend to have high job stability, making it a good role for an ESTJ. Senior accountants are in charge of handling all accounting operations within a company. They verify financial records and transactions, monitor junior accountants, and maintain account balances, financial statements, general ledgers, and all reports.

Public Relations Manager

Serving as the dedicated face of their company is perfect for the lead-by-example ESTJ. As public relations manager, the ESTJ will create and promote the public image of an organization. The PR Manager will select materials for release to various communications media and create and maintain a strong network of industry connections to increase organization awareness. They develop and implement communication strategies for media relations and make presentations as the public face of an organization.

Chief Executive Officer

There is no better role for an ESTJ than the title of CEO. The chief executive officer, also sometimes called the managing director, is the company leader. The CEO is in charge of all business operations and ventures. They report only to the board of directors. Ultimate responsibility for the success of the enterprise rests with the CEO, making ESTJs an excellent fit to handle this pressure and reward.

Business Development Manager

ESTJs are goal-oriented, making them successful business managers whose goal is to generate more revenue for the company. The business development manager is in charge of developing business solutions, managing client accounts, and coming up with new sales ideas. Creating new sales strategies, sales pitches, and business plans for success takes a strong leader who is ready to collaborate with their teammates, an ideal fit for an ESTJ.

Sales Manager

The sales manager role combines the natural talents of an ESTJ. The position requires managing both their sales team and the goods and services of the company. As a sales manager, the ESTJ will devise strategies for selling the product. This could mean establishing new sales territories, setting quotas, and coordinating new market disruptions.

Stock Trader

Aggressive, organized, and responsible, an ESTJ is well suited to a career as a stock trader. High-stakes financial transactions require a cool head. ESTJs have good judgment and are decisive, allowing them to handle the pressures of the stock market better than most.

Real Estate Agent

Real estate agents supervise the sale and purchase of homes, often managing multiple clients at once. This is no sweat for an ESTJ. Real estate agents must be organized and have an acumen for sales in order to act as an intermediary for buyers and sellers. They must study property listings, visit properties, and aggressively represent their clients.
